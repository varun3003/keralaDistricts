KERALA
Thiruvananthapuram
The capital city of Kerala, known for its historical significance and rich cultural heritage. Famous landmarks include the Padmanabhaswamy Temple and Kovalam Beach.
Kollam
An ancient port city on the Laccadive Sea coast known for its cashew industry and Ashtamudi Lake. It's a gateway to the backwaters of Kerala.
Pathanamthitta
Known for its religious tourism, it is home to the famous Sabarimala Temple. The district is also rich in natural beauty with its rivers and hills.
Alappuzha
Often referred to as the "Venice of the East," Alappuzha is famous for its picturesque backwaters and houseboat cruises. It is also known for its coir industry.
Kottayam
A land of letters, latex, and lakes, Kottayam is known for its contributions to literature and education. The Vembanad Lake and Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary are key attractions.
Idukki
Renowned for its mountainous terrain and wildlife sanctuaries, Idukki is home to the largest arch dam in Asia. Munnar, a popular hill station, is located here.
Ernakulam
The commercial capital of Kerala, Ernakulam is a bustling city known for its modern amenities and historical sites like Fort Kochi and the Jewish Synagogue.
Thrissur
Known as the cultural capital of Kerala, Thrissur hosts the famous Thrissur Pooram festival. The district is also home to several temples, churches, and cultural institutions.
Palakkad
Known as the "Rice Bowl of Kerala," Palakkad is famous for its paddy fields, Palakkad Fort, and the scenic Silent Valley National Park.
Malappuram
A district with a rich history and cultural heritage, Malappuram is known for its Islamic culture and educational institutions. The district is also famous for its scenic beauty.
Kozhikode
Formerly known as Calicut, this district is famous for its historic significance as a major trading port. Kozhikode is also known for its beautiful beaches and rich culinary traditions.
Wayanad
A lush, green district located in the Western Ghats, Wayanad is known for its wildlife sanctuaries, waterfalls, and spice plantations. It is a popular destination for nature lovers and trekkers.
Kannur
Known for its vibrant Theyyam ritual, Kannur is a district with a rich cultural heritage. The district also boasts beautiful beaches and historical landmarks like St. Angelo Fort.
Kasaragod
The northernmost district of Kerala, Kasaragod is known for its coir and handloom industries. The district is home to the famous Bekal Fort and picturesque beaches.
